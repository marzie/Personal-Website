Professional Biography

As a Senior Economist at the International Monetary Fund since 2013, I have extensive experience in international economics and working with governments of low-income, middle-income, and advanced economies. My work involves outreach to authorities of different countries on issues related to macro-finance and I have numerous public speaking experiences at high-level conferences and venues.

I have covered a wide range of economic issues including international accounts and balance of payment, monetary policy, financial sector supervision, labor market, budget and fiscal outlook, corporate and household economic activities, and real estate market analysis for both commercial and residential sectors.

I've led several research projects for the IMF including Nowcasting, Output Gap and monetary policy, and drivers of inflation. Prior to my current role, I completed doctoral internships at the International Monetary Fund, Norwegian Central Bank, and Minneapolis Federal Reserve Bank.

In 2015, I received the Big Data Contest Award ($50k) for proposing, designing, and building an advanced semantic search and browse tool that deploys state-of-the-art word-embedding technology to find relevant papers, authors, and concepts.

Research Focus

Monetary Policy & Financial Frictions

Examining the interplay between monetary policy trade-offs and financial frictions, with implications for central bank decision-making.

Nowcasting Economic Indicators

Developing advanced models for nowcasting GDP and other economic indicators using machine learning and novel data sources.

Output Gaps

Investigating financial factors and their implications for output gaps, especially during financial "booms and busts".

Phillips Curve Analysis

Analyzing the stability and dynamics of the Phillips Curve, particularly following global financial crises.
